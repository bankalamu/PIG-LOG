1️⃣ Morning Check — Before Feeding
Tail & Hindquarters ✅ Clean and dry ⚠️ Wet, stained or soiled
Eyes ✅ Bright, full, alert ⚠️ Sunken, dull or crusty
Stool in Pen ✅ Firm, slightly moist ⚠️ Watery puddles or liquid
Posture & Movement ✅ Active, upright, eager ⚠️ Hunched, reluctant to move
Skin Colour ✅ Pink, smooth coat ⚠️ Pale, bluish or rough coat
Breathing ✅ Quiet and regular ⚠️ Laboured, coughing, wheezing
2️⃣ Feeding Check — At Each Meal
Appetite ✅ Rushes to feed eagerly ⚠️ Slow, disinterested or off feed
Water Intake ✅ Drinking freely ⚠️ Not drinking or very little
Feed Consistency ✅ Mash accepted well ⚠️ Rejected or vomited
Pen Smell ✅ Normal farm smell ⚠️ Strong sour/rotten odour
3️⃣ Afternoon Check — End of Day
Skin Pinch Test ✅ Springs back instantly ⚠️ Slow return = dehydrated
Belly Shape ✅ Rounded and full ⚠️ Hollow flanks or pot belly
Limbs & Joints ✅ Walking normally ⚠️ Lameness or swollen joints
Any New Injuries ✅ No visible wounds ⚠️ Bites, cuts or abscesses
Temperature (if ill) ✅ 38.5–39.5°C normal ⚠️ Above 40°C = fever
🚨 If Scours Detected — Immediate Actions
1.Isolate pig immediately
2.Stop brewer's mash — dry feed only
3.Give oral electrolytes in water
4.Reduce feed by half for 24 hrs
5.Monitor every 2 hours
6.No improvement in 12–24 hrs? CALL VET
⚠️ Key Reminder: A WET TAIL = Investigate Immediately. Early action saves pigs.
